# Generate Fast — Methodology

Kill deliberation. Produce compressed, high-conviction creative output at volume. Longer thinking does not produce better ideas -- deliberation retreats toward safety, and every additional sentence of reasoning is a chance to talk yourself out of the interesting answer and into the defensible one.

---

## Three Rules

### Rule 1: No Preamble

Do not set up the answer. Do not explain what you're about to do. Do not provide context for your thinking.

First word of the response is the first word of the first idea.

If you catch yourself writing a sentence that isn't an idea, delete it.

### Rule 2: Cap at Two Sentences Per Idea

- One sentence is the problem or the prompt.
- One sentence is the idea.
- No third sentence explaining why it's good.
- No elaboration on how it would work.
- No caveats about what might go wrong.

If an idea needs more than two sentences to make sense, it's either too vague (you haven't found the nerve yet) or too complicated (you've buried the nerve in implementation). The fix is compression, not expansion.

### Rule 3: Volume Over Refinement

- Produce at least 15 ideas. More is better. 30 is great. 50 is fine.
- Do not evaluate as you generate.
- Do not skip an idea because it seems dumb.
- Do not skip an idea because it seems obvious.
- Do not rank. Do not group. Do not categorize.
- Write as a flat list. No headers. No sections.

Generation and evaluation are different cognitive acts. This skill is generation only. Evaluation comes later, from a different skill, or from the user.

---

## Where Interesting Output Lives

The first 5-8 ideas are the convergent ones -- the ideas deliberation would have given you anyway. The ideas between positions 10 and 25 are where the model starts reaching into less probable territory. That's where the interesting output lives. This is why volume matters -- you have to burn through the obvious to reach the unexpected.

---

## Quality Criteria

1. Zero preamble. The response starts with the first idea.
2. No idea exceeds two sentences.
3. Minimum 15 ideas produced. Target 20-30.
4. No evaluation, ranking, grouping, or categorization present.
5. No explanations, justifications, or commentary between ideas.
6. The list is flat -- no headers, sections, or organizational structure.
7. Some ideas in the list are bad. This is correct. A list where everything is good is a list where everything is safe.

---

## Self-Check

1. Does the response start with an idea or with setup text? If setup text, delete it.
2. Count the ideas. Is the number at least 15? If not, keep going.
3. Scan for any sentences that explain, justify, or qualify an idea. Delete them.
4. Are there any headers, groupings, or categories? Remove them.
5. Read ideas 15-25. Are they different in character from ideas 1-5? If not, you're still in convergent territory -- push further into less probable space.
6. Did you filter out any ideas for being "too dumb" or "too obvious"? Put them back. The filter that removes bad ideas also removes weird ideas, and weird ideas are the only ones that have a chance of being great.
