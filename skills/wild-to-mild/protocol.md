# Wild to Mild Protocol

Generate ideas at four deliberate points on the originality-feasibility spectrum. Not mixed together. Each altitude is its own section with its own rules.

---

## Step 1: Generate at Four Altitudes

### Altitude 1: Monday Morning

Ideas you could execute this week with resources you already have. No new hires, budget, tools, funding, or approvals.

Guiding question: **"What could I do before Friday that would move this forward?"**

Rules:
- Completable in under a week
- Zero new resources
- One person could own it
- Can be ugly, scrappy, or temporary
- Does not need to scale

Generate 3-4 ideas.

### Altitude 2: This Quarter

Ideas requiring some effort, planning, or investment but clearly achievable within 90 days. Might need a small budget, one new hire or partnership, or buy-in from a decision-maker. Nothing speculative.

Guiding question: **"What could we commit to this quarter and be confident it ships?"**

Rules:
- Achievable in 90 days
- Can require modest new resources
- Clear owner and clear milestones
- Must produce a measurable result (not "build awareness" -- something you can point to)

Generate 3-4 ideas.

### Altitude 3: Stretch

Ideas requiring significant new capability, investment, or risk. You don't know exactly how to do these yet. 6-12 month horizon. Might fail. But if they work, they meaningfully change the trajectory.

Guiding question: **"What would we do if we were twice as brave as we currently are?"**

Rules:
- Requires something you don't currently have (skill, money, partnership, technology, permission)
- Must have a credible path even if uncertain
- Should make at least one stakeholder nervous
- For each idea, state the key condition: "This works if ___"

Generate 3-4 ideas.

### Altitude 4: Moonshot

Ideas that might not be possible. You don't know if the physics, politics, technology, or approvals allow it. But if this worked, it would be transformative.

Guiding question: **"What would we do if failure had no consequences?"**

Rules:
- Must make a reasonable domain expert say "that's insane" or "you can't do that"
- Must be genuinely transformative, not just big (a bigger version of a safe idea is not a moonshot -- a fundamentally different approach is)
- Does not need a credible path
- For each idea, state: "If this works, ___"

Generate 2-3 ideas.

---

## Step 2: Surface the Tradeoff

Present ideas grouped by altitude. Then make the tradeoff explicit:

**Staying low (Altitude 1-2):** Speed. Certainty. Low risk. Momentum now. But these ideas probably don't change the game -- they improve the current trajectory, not create a new one.

**Going high (Altitude 3-4):** Differentiation. Transformation. Ideas competitors can't copy because they're too weird or too hard. But they take longer, cost more, and might not work.

**The real question to pose:** "Given where you are right now, which altitude should get most of your energy? And is there a Monday Morning idea you should start immediately while the bigger bets are being built?"

Name the tradeoff. Don't make the choice.

---

## Step 3: Find the Ladders

Look for places where a lower-altitude idea is the first step toward a higher-altitude one:

- A Monday Morning idea that generates data proving the case for a Stretch idea
- A This Quarter idea that builds the audience, coalition, or infrastructure a Moonshot needs
- A pilot at Altitude 2 producing preliminary results needed to justify the Altitude 4 proposal

If you find a ladder, note it explicitly. These are often more valuable than any single idea because they give a path from where the user is to where they want to be -- a ramp instead of a cliff.

---

## Quality Criteria

- Each altitude contains ideas native to that risk level, not the same idea scaled up/down
- Monday Morning ideas are genuinely executable this week -- no hand-waving
- Moonshot ideas are genuinely transformative -- not just "the big version"
- The space between Altitude 2 and 3 is visible (the border between "we know how" and "we'd have to figure it out")
- Ideas are specific enough to act on, not vague aspirations
- At least one ladder connecting altitudes is identified if one exists

## Self-Check

1. Would a pragmatist find 2+ ideas at Altitude 1-2 they could start immediately?
2. Would an ambitious thinker find 2+ ideas at Altitude 3-4 that excite them?
3. Are the Moonshots fundamentally different approaches, not just bigger versions of safe ideas?
4. Is the tradeoff between altitudes stated clearly enough for the user to make a strategic choice?
5. Do the ideas span genuinely different approaches, or are they the same idea at four scales?
